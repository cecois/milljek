Fast and dirty Sam Cooke story?

Cooke and sketchy lady friend speed away from a party to the Hacienda Hotel in L.A. (the location for this track, by the way); lady friend claims Cooke gets handsy, so she tiptoes her way outta there with a pocketful of Cooke's wad; Cooke stops around the grounds of La Hacienda scanning the darkness for his cash-strapped former companion, frightening the hotel manager to the point that she...shoots him down and bludgeons him with a wooden implement?! Yikes?!

Of course the best detail is Cooke's missing shoe and undershirtless sports coat, but let's show some respect, maybe? I guess?

Anyway, prior to that Cooke was responsible for other crimes, including not knowing much about history, biology, et al; begging Cupid for favors; and twistin' his nights away.

But he also put some heavy, kickass business down on tape. The heaviest? Probably "A Change is Gonna Come" in 1963. This is a muscular, anthemic (but surprisingly short) record that rightfully still carries a lot of the momentum of 1960s civil rights work.

"Change" is reported to be a response, at least in part, to an incident in Shreveport, La where Cooke and his entourage were initially booked at a Holiday Inn but refused a room upon arrival. Looking past the fact that they even wanted to stay at a Holiday Inn, which is funny, the open racial bastardry of those Holiday Innsters made Cooke, you know, pissed the fuck off. So he honked his horn and probably yelled and whatever else you do when you're denied something because of your race (I wouldn't know, I'm a whitey). And all the white desk clerks and middle managers panicked, of course, and called the cops and Cooke was shuffled off to the hoosegow.

Following this event, among others, Cooke finished off this frank, curt statement about the plight of African Americans in the U.S. up to the mid 1960s. It's especially interesting because it essentially says "watch the fuck out, folks," but does so within a lavish, acceptable arrangement.

I guess things have changed for the better? Your average Holiday Inn desk clerk isn't likely to engage in open racial hostility at least. (Aside: but what is your average Holiday Inn desk clerk likely to engage in, though? That's a hard species on which to draw a bead. They're almost always well-coifed and clean. Even prim. So you think they're okay because they behave like business students or party planners. But they're still, somehow, just...defeated. The rest of us wear our losses on our sleeves, kids. Why pretend everything is okay? If I'm booking a room at a Holiday Inn I already know things could be both better and worse, so let's just tone down the gold hair and the thin beards and behave like human beings, yes?)

Anyway, it's "A Change is Gonna Come," but not Sam Cooke's version with the strings and all of that bullshit. Strings ruin songs. Strings ruin songs. Most of the time, anyway, and this is one of them. Imagine how awesome this song would be if Cooke recorded it in a room full of percussionists. Just percussionists and Cooke's voice. Am I right?

Odd, then, that I would choose a version that's...strings! Or "string," more like it -- it's Ben Sollee doing this song with just a cello from Louisville's NuLuFest. It's pretty good, really. Nice and sparse and pretty powerful if you can handle hearing this come from a white NPR darling from Lexington, KY.
