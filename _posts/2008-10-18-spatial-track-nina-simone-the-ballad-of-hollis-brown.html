Remember that one time when Bob Dylan got intentionally obfuscational and impressionist and shot a fire full of holes? Well he's not always super fun like that. In "The Ballad of Hollis Brown" he drops some rural on us all:

There's seven breezes a-blowin'
All around the cabin door
There's seven breezes a-blowin'
All around the cabin door
Seven shots ring out
Like the ocean's pounding roar

Ok, ok, it's easy to drop a shotgun into a stanza for some immediate backwoods cred. But to have "seven breezes blowing" around the door? Actually poetic, Bob. And then comes the punchline. To wit:

There's seven people dead
On a South Dakota farm
There's seven people dead
On a South Dakota farm
Somewhere in the distance
There's seven new people born

...which Lyle Lofgren wrote "is probably the coldest piece of poetry I've ever heard." (Nevermind that ten new people were probably born, but let's leave the looming spectre of overpopulation and fresh water shortages out of this.

This first appeared on The Times They Are a-Changin' in 1964, just after The Freewheelin' Bob Dylan (1963 - "Blowin' in the Wind" and all) and I like to think of the Columbia Records exec hearing it and getting worried:

Exec

Bob, Bob, great stuff. Great stuff. Listen, the boys and I were wondering about the seven dead. Thinking maybe -- maybe that number could come down a little.

Dylan rambles on about a bunch of bullshit. Truth and whatnot

Exec

Sure, sure, babe. I get that. We get that. It's cool. Of course. It's just...yeah, that's all cool, babe, but maybe it could go a little more like Blowin', whaddya think? Just a little more, uh, hopeful, you see?

So you're getting "The Ballad of Hollis Brown", but guess what: it's a Nina Simone performance (from 1966's Let it All Out) instead of Dylan's. Why? Because Nina Simone is a kick-ass mama. Take it up with her.

And although Lofgren and others seem convinced (and are probably right) that the origin of the song could be traced to a true crime, I'm putting this one down right where it says -- rural South Dakota.
